Common Cyber Threats

Malware

Malicious software designed to damage or exploit your device. Includes viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware.

How to Protect:

  • Keep your antivirus software updated
  • Don't download from untrusted sources
  • Be cautious with email attachments
  • Regular system scans

Phishing

Fraudulent attempts to trick you into revealing sensitive information through fake emails or websites.

Warning Signs:

  • Urgent requests for personal information
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Suspicious links or attachments

Weak Passwords

Using simple passwords makes your accounts vulnerable to brute force attacks and unauthorized access.

Password Best Practices:

  • Use 12+ characters with mixed case
  • Include numbers and special characters
  • Never reuse passwords
  • Use a password manager

Unsecured Networks

Public Wi-Fi networks without encryption expose your data to interception and man-in-the-middle attacks.

Network Safety:

  • Use a VPN on public networks
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks
  • Keep your firewall enabled
  • Use HTTPS websites only

Social Engineering

Manipulative tactics to trick people into divulging confidential information or performing security-breaking actions.

Stay Protected:

  • Verify requests through official channels
  • Don't share personal information online
  • Be skeptical of unsolicited calls
  • Educate yourself on common tactics

Privacy Violations

Unauthorized collection and misuse of your personal data for identity theft, fraud, or targeted attacks.

Protect Your Privacy:

  • Review privacy settings regularly
  • Limit social media information
  • Use privacy-focused tools
  • Read privacy policies

Essential Safety Tips

1

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique, complex passwords with at least 12 characters including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

2

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security by requiring a second verification method beyond your password.

3

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browsers, and applications to patch security vulnerabilities.

4

Backup Your Data

Create regular backups of important files to protect against ransomware and data loss.

5

Use VPN on Public Networks

Encrypt your internet connection when using public Wi-Fi to prevent data interception.

6

Be Cautious with Emails

Verify sender identities, avoid clicking suspicious links, and never download unexpected attachments.

7

Use Antivirus Software

Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware tools to detect and remove threats.

8

Verify Website Authenticity

Check for HTTPS, look for security badges, and verify URLs before entering sensitive information.
